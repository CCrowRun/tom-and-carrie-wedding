Airports

Madison - Dane County Regional Airport (MSN)

  • Dane County Regional Airport is 15 minutes from Madison downtown.
  • Rental cars are available. Hotels provide courtesy shuttles for guests, and multiple taxi services are available for pickup.
  • Depending on the city, Madison may not have direct flights.
  • Due to the size of the airport, it can be expensive to fly in.

Milwaukee - General Mitchell International Airport (MKE)

  • MKE is 1 hour and 30 minutes from Madison downtown.
  • Rental cars are available. Greyhound has a route from MKE to Madison.
  • Depending on the city, Milwaukee may not have direct flights.
  • Due to the size of the airport, it can be expensive to fly in.

Chicago - O'Hare Airport (ORD)

  • ORD is 2 hour and 30 minutes from Madison downtown.
  • Rental cars are available. Megabus and Greyhound both have a route to Madison.
  • Typically has direct flights available.
  • Larger airport, typically with cheaper flights.

Driving Routes

From the East

Due to Indiana highways, the main route to Madison is on I-80/I-90W. The roads do include tolls, which would benefit from an I-Pass or EZPass. Typically traffic is at its worst around Chicago.

From the South

Unless you intend to stop in Chicago, if you are able to avoid the city you will avoid a majority of traffic. Taking I-39 North to Rockford could cut a large amount of time - typically traffic is limited that direction.

From the West

We have driven a number of routes from the West, and have found they are generally fairly similar. If you intend to stop along the way, we have recommendations in particular for Minneapolis, MN; Des Moines, IO; and Cedar Rapids IO.
